History of Slot Machines

Slot machines have been around for over a century and are one of the most popular forms of gambling in the world. They have gone through many changes since their inception from their original "Poker Machine" design to the modern video slot machines we see today. Here is a brief history of slot machines.

1891

The first slot machine was invented by Sittman and Pitt in Brooklyn, New York. It was a poker machine that had 5 drums with 50 playing cards. The machine was popular in bars and saloons, but it did not pay out money. Instead, players would win prizes like free drinks or cigars.

1963

Bally Technologies introduced the first electromechanical slot machine called Money Honey. It was the first machine to have a bottomless hopper and could pay out up to 500 coins without the need for an attendant. The machine was a huge success and paved the way for the modern slot machines we know today.

1976

The first video slot machine was invented by Fortune Coin Co. It was a modified version of a Sony TV and was placed in the Las Vegas Hilton hotel. The machine was a hit with players and quickly became popular in casinos around the world.

1996

IGT introduced the first progressive slot machine called Megabucks.
